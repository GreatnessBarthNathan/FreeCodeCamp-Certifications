Jürgen Norbert Klopp

The man who restored the glory of Liverpool FC

Here's a timeline of his life so far...

  • 1967 - Born in Stuttgart, Germany
  • 1990 - Was signed by Mainz 05, with his attitude and commitment making him a fan-favourite
  • 1995 - Moved to the position of center back to help the team, though he was signed as an attacker. That same year, Klopp obtained a diploma in sports science at the Goethe University of Frankfurt (MSc equivalent), writing his thesis about walking
  • 2001 - He retired as Mainz 05's record goal scorer, registering 56 goals in total, including 52 league goals
  • 2001 - Upon his retirement he was appointed as the club's manager. Klopp confessed that as a player he felt more suited to a managerial role, describing himself saying "I had fourth-division feet and a first-division head"
  • 2001-02 - Klopp guided Mainz to finish 4th in the league as he implemented his favoured pressing and counter-pressing tactics, narrowly missing promotion
  • 2003-04 - Klopp led Mainz to a third-place finish, securing promotion to the Bundesliga for the FIRST TIME in the club's history
  • 2004-05 - Despite having the smallest budget and the smallest stadium in the league, Mainz finished 11th in their first top-flight season
  • 2005-06 - Klopp's side finished 11th again in 2005-06 as well as securing qualification for the 2005-06 UEFA Cup
  • 2006-07 - Mainz 05 were relegated, but Klopp chose to remain with the club. However, unable to achieve promotion the next year, Klopp resigned at the end of the 2007-08 season
  • 2008 - Klopp resigned at the end of the 2007-08 season. He finished with a record of 109 wins, 78 draws and 83 losses
  • 2008 - In May 2008, Despite having interest from German champions Bayern Munich, Klopp eventually signed a two-year contract at the Dortmund. In his first season, Klopp won his first trophy with the club after defeating German champions Bayern Munich to claim the 2008 German Supercup
  • 2010-11 - Klopp's Durtmund team clinched the 2010-11 Bundesliga title, their seventh league title. Klopp's side were the youngest ever side to win the Bundesliga
  • 2011-12 - Klopp and his team successfully defended their title, winning the 2011-12 Bundesliga. Their total of 81 points that season was the greatest total points in Bundesliga history and the 47 points earned in the second half of the season also set a new record. Their 25 league wins equalled Bayern Munich's record, while their 28-league match unbeaten run was the best ever recorded in a single German top-flight season
  • 2012-13 - Klopp guided Dortmund to the Champions League Final
  • 2015 - Resigned from Durtmund. He finished with a record of 178 wins, 69 draws, and 70 losses
  • 2015 - Signed with liverpool and went on to reach the Carling Cup Final as well as the Europa League Final
  • 2016-17 - Finished Fourth in the premier league and qualified for the champions league, bringing Liverpool back to the competition after some time out
  • 2017-18 - Klopp led Liverpool to the final of the Champions League, which they lost 3-1 to Real Madrid
  • 2018-19 - Klopp led Liverpool to the final of the Champions League again, this time they won it, and it was their sixth victory in the competion.
  • 2019-20 - Led Liverpool to win their first Premier League title in 30years, and also won the Uefa Super Cup and the World Club cup - a trophy which Liverpool would win for the first time in their history
  • 2021-22 - Won both the EFL cup and FA cup with Liverpool, while reaching the Champions league with Liverpool and narrowly missing out of the premier league by just a single point.

We're champions of England, of Europe and the world. Five years ago I asked the supporters to believe us. I told the doubters to become believers, they made it happen.

—Klopp on Liverpool winning the Premier League in 2019-20, the club's first domestic title since 1990.
